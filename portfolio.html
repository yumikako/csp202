Falling Apples

The background of the apple tree and the hill will be drawn to start the game. In this project, the user will be asked to input a number from 1 through 6 to determine the amount of apples which will appear onto the tree. Then, it proceeds to ask the size of the apples which are small or large. The program then begins to draw the specified amount of apples in the corresponding size. The colors of each apples are randomized between red, green, and gold. Afterwards, the user is given a prompt to begin the apple drop which causes the apples to fall from the tree and bounce off the hill.

Spaceship

In this project, the user will fly around space controlling a spaceship using the arrow keys to get to the "planets" or the circular shapes seen on the screen. The player will maneuver the spaceship by moving it up and down to got forwards or backwards through the yse of PgUp and PgDn keys. The players will move left and right to move side to side using the end and home arrow keys. Once you have began moving the spaceship, use it to go around the screen and look for the circular planets. Once your spaceship comes in contact with the planets, you will gain a point. Once you have a total of ten points, the game will be finished.


Mancala

In this project, the objective of this game is to see which player can get the most stones into their pocket. One player will start the game by picking any pocket containing stones from their own side. The player will remove all the stones from that pocket, and deposit one stone at a time into neighboring pockets going counter-clockwise until the stones run out.If a player encounters their own store, a stone is deposited in it. If there are enough stones to go past the player’s own store, stones are deposited continuing on the other side’s pockets. However, if they encounter the other player’s store, that store is skipped over. If the last stone is deposited in the player’s own store, the player gets another turn. If the last stone is placed in an empty pocket on the player’s own side, the player takes this stone as well as the other player’s stones across from the empty pocket landed in, and places them in their own store. When all six pockets on one side are emptied the game ends. Each player will count the number of stones in their store. The player who has the most stones in their store wins.
